The Skill Ceiling

Players use in-game techniques of tapping on their keyboard to achieve max precision and accuracy as well as increasing the rate of which inputs are made.

these techniques include:

  • Indexing
  • Rolling
  • Teching(Rather more of a skill than a technique)

Indexing

indexing is a specialized finger-tapping technique for fast and dense sequences of beats. It is an advanced skill that allows players to alternate between just two fingers to hit patterns that would normally require multiple fingers, or to tap faster than they could with a single finger.

Why indexing is used :

  • Increased speed: By relying on only two fingers, players can achieve extremely high tapping speeds, as the physical travel distance for each finger is minimized.
  • Reduced stamina cost: Compared to rolling fingers across the keyboard, indexing can be a more efficient way to execute rapid inputs, conserving stamina for challenging sections.
  • Precision: Some players find that using this alternating two-finger technique gives them more control and consistency on certain patterns, leading to more accurate timing.

Rolling

"rolling" is an advanced technique where players use multiple fingers to quickly and repeatedly press keys on their keyboard for very fast, successive inputs. It is often used to handle extremely high-speed patterns and complex rhythms that are too fast for a single finger.

Instead of pressing a single button rapidly with one finger, rolling distributes the workload across multiple keys and fingers. For instance, you could press keys 1, 2, 3, 4 in a rapid sequence for four notes, or 1, 2, 1, 2 for a very fast two-note pattern. This technique is essential for tackling some of the most challenging, community-created levels.

Rolling is generally used for patterns that are too fast for a single-finger press, particularly fast streams and bursts. It is distinct from single-finger tapping and indexing:

  • Single-tapping involves using one finger to hit every note.
  • Indexing is a slightly faster technique that uses two fingers to alternate hits, like 1, 2, 1, 2.
  • Rolling is an extension of indexing that typically involves three or more fingers rolling across the keys, like 1, 2, 3, 4 or 1, 2, 3, 1, 2, 3, to handle even higher speeds.

Teching

"teching" refers to playing custom levels that utilize advanced and unconventional level design techniques, known as "tech". These levels go beyond the standard rhythm and patterns found in the official game and often require players to use multiple fingers and adapt to new charting styles.

Key characteristics of tech levels:

  • Unconventional charting: Tech levels introduce rhythms, tempos, and shapes that are not typical in classic ADOFAI levels. They push the boundaries of the level editor to create novel and sometimes unintuitive challenges.
  • Use of multi-hits: One popular tech technique involves creating "multi-hit tiles." The game does not officially support these, so they are made by rapidly combining 179.9 and 180-degree tiles. This forces the player to tap multiple times on what appears to be a single tile.
  • Advanced level editor features: Teching heavily relies on exploiting the game's level editor. The technique for multi-hit tiles, for instance, uses "move track" events to hide certain tiles, creating the illusion of more complex and faster patterns.
  • Learning alternative inputs: To navigate the complex and rapid sequences in tech levels, players often need to move beyond single-key input. The community has developed techniques like "indexing" and "rolling" to use multiple keys on the keyboard to handle the intricate patterns.
  • High skill and community focus: Because tech levels feature advanced and community-popularized design ideas, they are typically aimed at more advanced players. The ADOFAI community has been vital in developing and popularizing these techniques. The developers have officially recognized this community movement and added curated "Tech Wave" levels to the game.
